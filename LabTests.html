Lab Tests

Complete blood count

A Complete Blood Count (CBC) test is done to get information on various kinds of blood cells, such as red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets.

A CBC test helps the doctor detect any bruising, fatigue, or weakness in the body. Other health conditions such as anemia, infections can also be detected with the help of a CBC test.

Urine Routine Test

A urine R/E (Urine Routine/Examination or Routine Urine Analysis) is used to diagnose and treat various illnesses, including urinary tract infections, kidney disease, and diabetes.

During a urinalysis, the concentration, appearance, and content of urine are examined. A urinary tract infection, for example, might cause urine to appear hazy. Increased protein levels in urine can indicate renal dysfunction.

Fasting Blood Glucose

The fasting blood sugar test evaluates the levels of blood glucose after an 8-hour overnight fast.

Glucose is derived from carbohydrates and in the presence of insulin, it enters the cell to be converted to energy. When fasting blood glucose levels are elevated, it indicates a risk of diabetes.

C-Reactive Protein (CRP)

C-reactive protein is a substance that the liver produces as a response to inflammation.

This test is conducted to measure the amount of protein in the blood. The test helps in diagnosing the acute and chronic conditions that are caused due to inflammation.

Liver Function Test (LFT)

A liver function test (LFT) consists of a group of tests that are done to understand the overall health of the liver.

During the liver function test, various enzyme and protein levels are measured. The proteins that are present in the liver perform functions such as energy generation, detoxification, and purification of blood.

Lipid Profile

A lipid profile test is used to determine the level of cholesterol and triglycerides in the blood.

Cholesterol and triglycerides are fats in the body, and they perform vital functions. If they build up in your blood, it can be harmful to your health. Excess quantities of lipids in the blood will lead to clogged and narrowed arterial passages.
